Hello, my name is Michelle Ocegueda, a current undeclared UCR undergraduate student. Although I have not chosen a major yet, I have had many experiences in various fields. Despite having been in a STEM program for six years in middle and high school, I am much more interested in Humanities and Social Sciences. I have learned and honed many soft skills during my time as a student and other extracurricular activities. If I do not know how to do something, I am determined to learn. My job experience is limited, however I have extensive volunteering experience ranging from helping out in an office setting to marathons.

My time in the 49ers SLI Program opened the doors to the STEM world for me. Because of this opportunity, I was able to experience things I wouldn't have. However, I realized I was more interested in social sciences and helping people out. Volunteering became a significant part of my high school life as I started helping out at my local parish office regularly after school for about six months. There, I learned about many things and skills needed in an office setting. My tasks changed from time to time depending on what the office director and other employees needed help with. I would help with data input, prepping mail and documents, emailing families, attending to visitors, and other small jobs. I have only had one part-time job at a small medical supplies company, but I did different types of tasks, allowing me to learn many things about documentation, inspections, and packaging products.

With the experiences and knowledge that I have gained, I wish to help people out. My goal is to become a social worker, in order to make things easier for others seeking some form of help. Me being fluent in Spanish and English is also a skill that I hold dear, especially after witnessing many times how important it is for people to feel understood. As I am the eldest daughter of four, to two immigrants with little to zero knowledge in the English language, my job as a translator was basically given to me at birth. I owe a lot of my skills and traits to my family and life at home. Being the oldest of four daughters, has made me a patient, open-minded, caring, and adaptive person. Even though my anxiety has oftentimes made it difficult for me to do and try many things, I have learned to cope with it in different ways, in order to allow myself to experience new opportunities.
